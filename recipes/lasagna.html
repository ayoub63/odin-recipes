Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish known for its layers of pasta, rich sauce, and melted cheese. It's a hearty and comforting meal that's perfect for feeding a crowd or enjoying as leftovers.
Typically, lasagna consists of alternating layers of lasagna noodles, marinara sauce, a mixture of ricotta and Parmesan cheese, and mozzarella cheese.
Additional ingredients such as ground beef or Italian sausage may be included in the sauce for added flavor and protein.
The dish is baked in the oven until the noodles are tender and the cheese is golden and bubbly.
Lasagna is a beloved classic that's sure to please both family and friends.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Marinara sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Parmesan cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Ground beef or Italian sausage (optional)
  • Onions (optional)
  • Garlic (optional)
  • Herbs and spices (optional, e.g., basil, oregano)

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions until al dente.
  3. In a skillet, brown the ground beef or Italian sausage (if using) over medium heat. Drain any excess fat, then add onions and garlic to the skillet and cook until softened.
  4. Spread a thin layer of marinara sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  5. Place a layer of cooked lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  6. Spread a layer of ricotta cheese over the noodles, followed by a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese and mozzarella cheese.
  7. Add a layer of the meat sauce mixture (if using).
  8. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used, ending with a layer of sauce and cheese on top.
  9. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake in the preheated oven for about 45 minutes.
  10. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  11. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
  12. Enjoy your delicious homemade lasagna!
